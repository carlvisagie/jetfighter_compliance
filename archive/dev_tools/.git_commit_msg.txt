vio(L2): pictogram vocabulary + multi-expressive spine (river chokes on stalls, breaks at critical findings)

Carl 2026-06-05:
  "go hog wild with your imagination... the timeline itself must convey
   a myriad of information and situations -- thicker lines for some
   things thinner for others a broken line for some a choked out line
   in some cases whatever the imagination can come up with to help tell
   the story without words text or old tech"

This commit upgrades L2 in two simultaneous directions: the events
become rich pictograms, and the spine line itself gains stall- and
break-aware geometry.

EVENT SHAPES -> PICTOGRAMS

  Replaced the geometric primitive vocabulary (rect, triangle, hex,
  star) with recognizable pictograms. Each event renders as a small
  <g> of multi-path SVG that an operator reads at a glance:

    paper       -> folded-corner page with 3 ruling lines
    paper-gen   -> same page with a green lightning bolt (organism-made)
    gap         -> dashed ghost page with a `?` (missing slot)
    issue       -> warning triangle with `!`
      .urgent   -> flame
      .deadline -> hourglass
      .note     -> speech bubble
    phase       -> hexagon (echoes the stage backbone) + green check
    milestone   -> 5-point green star
    confirmation-> open amber circle with `?`
    payment     -> envelope outline (link sent)
      .paid     -> dollar coin
    finding     -> warning triangle (or stop-sign octagon when critical)
    broker      -> finish flag on a pole

  Per-kind CSS tints the pictogram by severity (priority="high" gap
  goes red, finding severity="critical" becomes a red stop-sign, etc.)
  so colour also carries the meaning.

SPINE LINE -> MULTI-EXPRESSIVE

  _drawSpineRiver now folds two additional signals into the river
  geometry (still one cheap <path> + a handful of overlays):

    BUSY  -> SWELLS (existing density-driven thickness)
    QUIET -> NARROWS
    STALL -> CHOKES   (NEW: any inter-event gap >=24h collapses the
                       river to a 0.35px hairline through that window;
                       a stalled intake now visibly differs from a
                       busy one)
    BREAK -> SCAR     (NEW: critical/high findings render a red X-mark
                       on top of the river at their X; reads "this is
                       where it broke" without a single word)

  Plus a STATIC live-tip glow at the rightmost end of the past
  river. Static, not animated -- motion discipline says continuous
  motion is only for unresolved demands, and recent activity is not
  a demand.
