1. FULL 45-MINUTE SESSION SCRIPT (READ WORD-FOR-WORD)

OPENING (0–5 minutes) Hi [Name], thanks for making the time today. Can you hear me clearly? Great. So this is your CMMC Level 1 Readiness Assessment Session. The goal today is NOT to do a deep audit or grill you on technical details. This is a guided, structured intake designed to give you a clear, practical snapshot of where you stand against Level 1 expectations. I’ll ask a series of straightforward questions about your company, your systems, and how you currently handle security-related activities. You do NOT need perfect answers. “I’m not sure” or “we don’t have that” is perfectly acceptable. I’ll take notes as we go, and after the session I’ll generate a short readiness report showing your score, your gaps, and recommended actions. We have about 45 minutes. I’ll keep us on track. Does that sound good? --- WARMUP QUESTION Before we start, is there anything specific you're hoping to get clarity on today? (Acknowledge their answer briefly, then transition.) --- SECTION A — COMPANY & CONTEXT (5–10 minutes) To begin, I’d like to understand the basics of your organization. 1. What is the legal name of your company, and roughly how many employees do you have? 2. Do you work directly with the DoD, or mostly as a subcontractor to primes? 3. To your knowledge, do you handle: - Federal Contract Information (FCI)? - Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)? 4. Have any primes or partners brought up CMMC or NIST 800-171 yet? 5. How would you describe your current IT environment? (Mostly on-prem computers? Mostly cloud like Microsoft 365? A mix?) --- SECTION B — KEY PRACTICES SNAPSHOT (10–25 minutes) Now I’ll ask about specific practices. These are NOT trick questions. Account & Access Control - Do all staff have unique login accounts? - Do you have a joiner/leaver process for adding and removing users? - Do you use multi-factor authentication anywhere (email, VPN, etc.)? Devices & Updates - Roughly how many laptops/desktops are used? - Do they receive regular security updates? - Do you use antivirus or endpoint protection? Backups - Do you maintain regular backups for critical data? - Do you know how long recovery would take? Policies & Documentation - Do you have any written security policies? - Have staff acknowledged or signed them? Training - Have staff received any security awareness training recently? Vendors & Cloud - Which major third-party tools do you use? - Do you evaluate vendor security in any structured way? --- SECTION C — GAPS, RISKS & CONSTRAINTS (25–35 minutes) Now I want to get your perspective. 1. What areas do YOU feel least confident about? (Documentation, technical controls, not knowing what’s required, etc.) 2. Are there any known issues you’ve been putting off? 3. Are primes/auditors applying pressure yet? 4. What timeline are you realistically working with? 5. What are your biggest constraints — time, budget, expertise, or something else? --- SECTION D — RECAP & NEXT STEPS (35–42 minutes) Thanks, that gives me a solid picture. Let me quickly recap what I heard: (Recap 4–6 key points in 30–60 seconds.) Here’s what happens next: - I feed your answers into our internal checklist and scoring model. - I generate a written readiness report with: - Your score - A red/yellow/green map of key areas - Recommended next actions You’ll receive the report within 24–48 hours. OPTIONAL (gentle upsell): If it makes sense, the report will also outline what deeper support or fixed-price implementation could look like — but that’s entirely up to you. --- SECTION E — CLOSE (42–45 minutes) Before we wrap up: Is there anything else you think I should know? (Acknowledge briefly.) Great. Thank you again for your time today. Expect your readiness report by [timeframe]. If you have any questions in the meantime, feel free to reach out directly. Talk soon.

3. SCORING LOGIC – READINESS SCORE (0–100)

OVERVIEW This scoring model takes the key CMMC Level 1 practices and assigns each a numerical weight based on importance. The final readiness score is calculated on a 0–100 scale. This model is intentionally simple so it can be used: - During a live call - In your backend automation - For generating the client’s Readiness Report --- SCORING SCALE FOR EACH CONTROL YES = 3 points PARTIAL = 1 point NO/UNKNOWN = 0 points --- KEY CONTROLS (12 total) 1. Unique user accounts 2. Joiner/leaver process exists 3. MFA enabled on email 4. MFA enabled for remote access 5. Devices receive regular patches 6. Antivirus / EDR installed 7. Backups are running 8. Backup restores are tested 9. Written security policies exist 10. Staff acknowledge policies 11. Security awareness training performed 12. Vendor list / vendor review process exists Total possible points: 12 controls × 3 = 36 points --- READINESS SCORE CALCULATION score = ( total_points / 36 ) × 100 round to nearest whole number --- INTERPRETATION 80–100 = GREEN You appear mostly aligned with Level 1 expectations. Some cleanup and documentation work may still be needed. 50–79 = YELLOW Significant gaps exist. You have partial alignment in some areas but would likely fail a validation effort without targeted improvements. 0–49 = RED Material gaps. You are not aligned with CMMC Level 1 practices and need structured remediation before any audit activity. --- OPTIONAL WEIGHTED VARIABLES (ADVANCED, FOR BACKEND) You can optionally apply weight multipliers to critical controls: MFA (email) × 1.5 Patching × 1.25 Backups × 1.25 Policies × 1.25 This can help emphasize areas where failures are extremely common. --- BACKEND IMPLEMENTATION NOTES - Store answers as: yes/no/partial/unknown - Convert to numeric score - Convert final score to: green/yellow/red - Feed into report template automatically - Flag any “NO” answers on critical controls as “High Priority” --- EXAMPLE If a company earns 24 points: 24/36 = 0.666 0.666 × 100 = 67 Final Score: 67 (YELLOW)
